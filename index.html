Bio

Arya is the youngest daughter of Lord Eddard and Lady Catelyn Stark. She is headstrong, more interested in swordplay than crochet and bored by the rituals of courtly life. She worships her father and is close to half-brother Jon (as parting gift before joining the Night’s Watch, he presented her with a sword, Needle). Like the Bastard of Winterfell, Arya has always thought of herself as an outsider – a young woman who’d rather forge her own destiny than abide by the conventions of her class and marry a rich husband.

Accomplishments

Growing up in the grey, windswept north, Arya was a rebel with a cause. She wanted to prove to her brothers she was as tough and smart as they.Her freedom proved short-lived as she was snatched by The Hound, who liked the idea of ransoming Arya to the Starks . Their destination was The Twins and the wedding of her brother Robb. Striking up an unlikely buddy-movie relationship, Arya and The Hound reached the outskirts of the keep just as House Bolton had betrayed the Starks and turned the nuptials into a slaughter. Later, Arya killed a soldier who joked about sewing a dire wolf's head onto Robb Stark's body – the first occasion she consciously took a life.

She had crossed the Narrow Sea to Braavos, a Venice-like warren watched over by a giant iron Titan. She was en route to the House of Black and White, hoping to resume acquaintances with shape-shifting assassin Jaqen H'ghar.

The next we saw of Arya, she was deftly beheading a pigeon for supper. Unfortunately, Braavos was the not the sort of city where you simply walked around swinging a headless pigeon and she was quickly surrounded by local hoodlums. When an apparent doppleganger of the grumpy gatekeeper appeared the bullies fled. Back at the House of Black and White he removed his hood and, by the time the camera panned all the way behind Arya, had assumed the likeness of Jaqen. "Who are you?" Arya asked (she seemed to have dropped her pigeon in the excitement). "No one," he replied. "And that is what a girl must become." In the background, a sitar twanged exotically – a patented Game of Throne reminder we're not in dank, straightforward Westeros any more, Toto.

Important Moments

Witnessing Her Father's Execution

Arya's entire trajectory in life altered with the public beheading of Eddard Stark. With his passing, something inside her died too.

Taking Revenge on Poliver

Arya took her time dispatching the Lannister thug early in season four, sliding Needle into his throat so that he gargled his own blood – a repeat of the sadistic method by which he had dispatched her friend Lommy.

Leaving The Hound to Die

The Stark girl's transition from naive tom-boy to cold-hearted survivor was sealed as she abandoned her companion with a fatal wound, ignoring his pleas for mercy.

The Red Wedding

Arya was powerless to save her mother and brother from the treacherous Boltons. There was nothing she could do – and yet her helplessness continues to haunt her.
